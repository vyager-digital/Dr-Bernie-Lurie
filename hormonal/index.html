Why You're Still Suffering

The Root Causes Most Doctors Never Investigate

In my experience, hormonal imbalance is never random — it has traceable, addressable causes. The question isn't whether you can heal. It's whether anyone has looked for what's actually causing it.

Nutritional Deficiencies

Hormones are built from nutrients. Iodine deficiency devastates thyroid function. Magnesium drives 300+ hormonal reactions. Zinc is essential for progesterone production.

Environmental Toxins

Xenoestrogens in plastics, pesticides, and personal care products mimic estrogen in the body — disrupting the delicate hormonal orchestra that governs your entire cycle.

Adrenal Exhaustion

Chronic stress floods the body with cortisol. Over time, adrenal fatigue steals the building blocks for sex hormones, leaving women depleted, anxious, and exhausted.

Gut Dysbiosis

The estrobolome — gut bacteria that metabolize estrogen — when disrupted, leads to estrogen dominance or deficiency. Gut health and hormonal health cannot be separated.
